Anti-xSxJ Manifesto

"A system optimized only for one type is a system that malfunctions for the rest."

TL;DR

This manifesto is not a hate speech. It is a structured critique of the overrepresentation and normative dominance of the xSxJ cognitive type in organizational, educational, and societal systems.

If you're an xSxJ, this is not personal. But if you believe your sense of "common sense" should govern everyone, this is about you.

Why This Exists

We live in a world designed by and for the xSxJ.
A world where:

  • "Unwritten rules" are treated as law.
  • "Common sense" is just the local logic of the majority.
  • "Predictability" is considered virtue, and "non-linearity" is framed as immaturity.
  • Being "on time" is more important than being "right."
  • Deviation is penalized before it's understood.

This manifesto exists because those of us who operate on intuition, possibilities, and creative deviation are systemically punished for not conforming to a structure we never agreed to.

Core Statement

  • That "common sense" is a political technology, not a moral truth.
  • That prioritizing Sensing-Judging cognitive styles in policies, workflows, and cultures creates systemic exclusion.
  • That the diversity of cognition is not optional — it is essential for any adaptive, intelligent society.
  • That Ne, Ni, Ti, Fi — and all other functions — deserve equal architectural consideration in societal systems.

Goals

  • To refactor environments so they support multiple MBTI cognitive styles, not just one.
  • To make unwritten rules obsolete, replaced with transparency and opt-in agreements.
  • To shift from obedience-based trust models to autonomy-based collaboration.
  • To advocate for systems that celebrate deviation as innovation, not defect.

What This Is Not

  • This is not a call to erase xSxJ people. It is a call to stop designing systems as if only they exist.
  • This is not about personality wars. It's about infrastructural oppression disguised as etiquette.
